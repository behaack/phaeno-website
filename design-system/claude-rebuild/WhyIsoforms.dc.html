Why RNA Isoforms?

Gene-level counts compress biology

Isoforms capture splicing, promoter usage, UTR variation, and transcript-specific regulation — often the true functional drivers that gene-level summaries obscure.

PSeq exposes the molecular phenotype of each sample, revealing the biological drivers hidden behind incomplete isoform resolution.

The failure of genome sequencing to provide a direct map between genotype and phenotype is well established. Closing that gap requires profiling every RNA arising from every gene, comprehensively, accurately, and economically — which is exactly what PSeq was built to do.
